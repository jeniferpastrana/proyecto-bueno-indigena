    "The Embrace of the Serpent" is a Colombian film directed by Ciro Guerra, released in 2015. The story takes place in the Colombian Amazon and follows two timelines separated by several decades. The main plot revolves around Karamakate, an Amazonian shaman and the last survivor of his tribe, who guides two European explorers at two different times. The first is in the early 20th century, when Karamakate helps German ethnobotanist Theodor Koch-Grunberg in his search for a sacred plant. The second is in the 1940s, when Karamakate accompanies an American ethnobotanist, Richard Evans Schultes, on a similar mission.
